Made for VPNs that don't run on your device

A router, a Tailscale exit node, a travel router, a home server — when the tunnel lives somewhere else, your iPhone or Mac has no built-in way to know it's still up.

No on-device switch to check

When your VPN runs on your network instead of in an app, there's no toggle on your phone showing green. Egress checks your actual public egress from the device you're using — so you can see the tunnel is really carrying your traffic.

These setups fail quietly

Routers reboot, exit nodes drop, you wander onto another Wi-Fi network, a config breaks — usually with no sign anything changed. Egress keeps your real egress on screen, so a silent failure is right there at a glance instead of going unnoticed.

Confirmed at the source — not guessed

Egress doesn't infer your location from your IP. It verifies the thing that actually matters: are you exiting through the egress you expect?

For supported providers

Egress asks the provider's own connection check — the same one they host on their site to tell you "you are connected." An authoritative answer, surfaced right in your widget.

For everything else

Add a Custom Egress and match against the public IP ranges you define. Use it for an unlisted VPN — or to confirm you're on your work or home network before you assume you are.

Built to be glanced at, and trusted

You don't have to think about it.

Glance, don't open

Pin the widget and get passive, at-a-glance confirmation — no launching, no tapping, no logging in. One look tells you whether you're still covered, before you assume you are.

iPhone, iPad, and Mac

Home-screen widget on iPhone and iPad, a desktop widget or menu-bar item on Mac. Your custom setups sync privately over iCloud. Set it up once.

Private by design

No tracking, no accounts, no phoning home. Egress is fully open-source — so "no tracking" is something you can verify, not just take our word for. That's rather the point.
