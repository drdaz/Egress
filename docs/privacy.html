Privacy Policy

Last updated: 16 June 2026

The short version: Egress does not collect, transmit, or store your personal data. There are no accounts, no analytics, no trackers, and no servers operated by us. Because Egress is open-source, you don't have to take our word for it — you can read the code.

Who we are

Egress is a free, open-source utility for iPhone and Mac that confirms whether your traffic is exiting through the VPN or network you expect. This policy explains how the app handles data. It applies to the Egress apps and this website.

What we collect

Nothing. Egress has no developer-operated servers and no backend. We do not collect, receive, log, or store any personal information about you. We have no way to identify you and no account for you to create.

Network requests the app makes

To do its job, Egress makes network requests directly from your device to third-party services. These requests do not pass through any server of ours — we don't operate one. But by the nature of any internet request, the service you contact will see your device's public IP address. We don't control these services, and each is governed by its own privacy policy.

  • Determining your public IP (Custom Egress). To check a Custom Egress, Egress needs your device's current public IP address. It requests this from a third-party lookup service, ipwho.is, which returns your IP address and an approximate location (such as country and city). Egress then compares that IP against the ranges you defined — the comparison happens on your device. Egress sends ipwho.is nothing that identifies you beyond the request itself.
  • Provider connection checks (supported providers). When you select a supported provider, Egress queries that provider's own connection-check endpoint — for example am.i.mullvad.net (Mullvad), api.ivpn.net (IVPN), or airvpn.org (AirVPN). The request goes directly from your device to that provider, which reports whether you're connected along with details such as your IP and the server's location. These are governed by the provider's own privacy policy.

The complete, current list of endpoints Egress contacts is visible in the open-source code. Egress makes no advertising or analytics requests of any kind.

What's stored on your device

  • The egress you've selected and any Custom Egress definitions you create are stored locally on your device.
  • If you are signed in to iCloud, your settings may sync across your devices through Apple's iCloud (key-value storage). This data is handled by Apple under Apple's privacy policy; it is not sent to us and we cannot access it.

No tracking

Egress contains no third-party analytics, advertising, or tracking SDKs. It does not build a profile of you, does not track you across apps or websites, and requests no advertising identifier.

Children

Egress does not collect personal data from anyone, including children.

This website

This site is hosted on GitHub Pages and loads no third-party fonts, analytics, or trackers. GitHub may collect standard server logs (such as IP addresses) as part of serving the page; see GitHub's privacy statement for details.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the updated version will be posted here with a new date above. Because the site is open-source, every change is also visible in the project's version history.
