Frequently asked questions

What does Egress do?

Egress is a widget that confirms whether your traffic is exiting through the VPN or network you expect. For supported providers it reads the provider's own "you are connected" check; for anything else, it matches your public IP against ranges you define.

Which providers are supported?

Egress supports several VPN providers out of the box, and a Custom Egress option that works with any VPN or network you can identify by its public IP ranges. Missing a provider? Request it on GitHub.

Does it work on iPhone and Mac?

Yes. Egress runs on both, with a home-screen widget on iOS and a menu-bar item on macOS. Your custom setups sync across your devices over iCloud.

Is it really free?

Yes — free and open-source, with no subscription, paywall, or ads. You can read the full source on GitHub.

Does Egress collect my data?

No. Egress has no servers, accounts, or trackers, and collects nothing about you. See the Privacy Policy for the details.

It says I'm not connected, but I am — what gives?

The current version matches your exit using IPv4. On an IPv6-only network the check is handled gracefully rather than matched, so a result may be inconclusive; full IPv6 matching is on the way. If you think something else is wrong, please open an issue with the details.

Contributing

Egress is open-source. Bug reports, provider requests, and pull requests are welcome on GitHub.
